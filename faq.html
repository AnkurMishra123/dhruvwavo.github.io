Lenders offer a variety of products. You should compare the numerous offers given by all of the banks and NBFCs to determine which lender is best for you. It is vital to conduct thorough research on the interest rates provided, processing costs assessed, for various tenures, and further comprehend the Loan-to-Value ratios of each lender. Additionally, keep an eye out for any special lending campaigns that could be going on. You can also verify your eligibility with other institutions to find out how much of a house loan you can be eligible for. This aids in your search for the ideal house loan for your needs.

The most popular loan option for buying a home is a home loan. Depending on your financial institution, you may be able to obtain financing for 80% – 90% of the market value of the home.

Home Loan Balance Transfer - By choosing to transfer your home loan, you can stabilise your finances. Refinancing your current home loan is simpler than you would think, whether you're trying to reduce your EMIs or consolidate your debts.
Loan against property - If you want to use your personal or commercial property as security for the financing, this is exactly what you need.
Build a home that satisfies all of your demands with a plot + construction loan from our specialised selection of home loans.
Renovation + Extension Loan – Home Improvement Loan will enable you to repair or renovate your house so that it meets the needs of you and your family as a contemporary place to live.
House Loan Top Up - You can choose home top-up loans if you currently have a home loan but need additional money. The interest rates are significantly lower than those for personal loans generally.

When determining whether you are eligible for a loan, the following elements are taken into account:
  • • Age
  • • Place of residence
  • • Income per year
  • • Financial stability
  • • Age of the Co-Applicant
  • • Income of co-applicants
  • • Credit Score
  • • Any debts or loans that are now owed

Home loan providers keep a buffer to cover their costs and do not lend 100% of the value of the property. The lender may provide the home buyer a loan for up to 90% of the property's worth. The buyer would be forced to put down the remaining money.
